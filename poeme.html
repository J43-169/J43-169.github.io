The Crow

Poème

Auteur: Nina Billard Sarmadi

I once saw a crow.
It froze into the night like the rough strings of a tapestry,
And I saw its eyes, two new moons, painted with stars.
It ran from tree to tree, jaggedly, like a highwayman fleeing on his horse.


I once saw a crow in plain daylight.
It was clawing soft, damp turf.
It was stout and black, with a long strong beak.
It could have been the shadow of a kingfisher,
Or the kingfisher its shadow on another world.


I once heard a crow on a January night.
Its cries echoed in the air,
Like two pieces of tarnishing silver, rubbing together,
Rolling over the valleys of thick, dark night.
